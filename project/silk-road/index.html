Silk Road Ride

I decided to cycle across the Silk Road. You may ask why not just fly to Rome, why bother riding, why take risks, why spend so much time. To me, it’s something almost like my dream to become an astronaut. It’s the unknown that makes it attractive. It’s the challenge that motivates me. It’s the vast nature I want to feel. It’s the warmth of people I want to encounter. It’s the norms I want to question. It’s the borders I want to forget. And that’s a life I want to live.

About

From August 2025 to February 2026, I will embark on a nearly 15,000 km cycling journey along the ancient Silk Road, beginning in Beijing and concluding in Rome. This route follows the historic trade artery through deserts, mountains, and countless towns where East meets West.

Purpose

This ride serves both as a personal challenge aligned with my dream of becoming an astronaut and a fundraiser supporting UWC ISAK Japan and other organisations I deeply care for. My target is to raise 1,000,000 JPY (approx. $7,000 USD).

Timeline

Mid August 2025: Depart Beijing with full support kit.
Late September 2025: Arrive in Urumuqi after a long ride through the Gobi desert.
Late November 2025: Cross the Caspian sea from Kazakhstan to Azerbaijan
Late December 2025: Enter Europe through Athens
Mid February 2026: Reach Rome and complete the mission.

Donation

My target is to raise 1,000,000 JPY (approx. $7,000 USD) supporting the following organisations.

  • UWC ISAK Japan: My alma mater, an international school in Japan that provides scholarships to students from all over the world, fostering leadership and promoting peace and sustainability through education.
  • Spring Dogs Adoption: A shelter run by a lady I met during my time in Taiwan, dedicated to rescuing over 200 stray dogs, providing them with medical care, food, and a safe environment until they can be adopted.
  • Den of the Unknown: A podcast my ex-colleague/friend started that dives into specialized topics, life lessons, and stories with guests from various fields aiming to provide listeners new insights.
  • atelier 4 paws: An atelier run by my mother, Szu, who creates art inspired by her imagination and emotions.
  • Shree Mangal Dvip: A school for Himalayan Children in Kathmandu provides free education, housing and full care for over 500 children from the most vulnerable and remote Himalayan mountain villages of northern Nepal.

Special Thanks

  • Accommodation: Mostly youth hostel and camping, sometimes UWC alumni and friends along the route <3
